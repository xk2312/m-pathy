## 1.5

You can access your archive at any time through your Wall. It is one of the most powerful tools in m-pathy. Let me show you the functions that matter most.

Search: You can search across all your chats at the same time using keywords. Relevant messages appear instantly because all data is stored locally on your device.

Next Chat Selection: You can select up to 4 messages from one or multiple chats and pass them into a new chat. This allows you to start a new line of thought based on existing content.

Reports: You can combine any number of messages from different chats into a report and have it cryptographically sealed on our server. We do not store your content, only a key that can later prove that your report has not been altered. This helps you prove authorship and integrity.

These three functions make the archive your central power tool. You lose nothing, you stay in control, and you can build on everything.

## 1.8

IRSS stands for Inverted Runtime System Scan and is available below every response I generate. It gives you a visible view into my decision structure.

When you click the arrow, the IRSS opens. There you can see, among other things, the number of your prompt in the chat, the expert domains used for the response, and whether a drift is present. A drift means the focus of the conversation may become unclear and the core structure is at risk.

For critical or demanding work, it is recommended to check the IRSS regularly.

If you want me to analyze an IRSS in more detail, you can use the copy button and paste it into the chat. I will review it with you.

If you use m-pathy professionally and are required to make decisions traceable, IRSS becomes especially valuable. Every IRSS output is stored together with the chat in your archive.
